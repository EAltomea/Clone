Overview

A Deep Connection to Nature and Tradition

The Philippines is home to diverse Indigenous communities that preserve rich cultural traditions, social systems, and beliefs. One of the largest Indigenous groups in Mindanao is the Manobo tribe. The Manobo people are known for their deep connection to nature, strong community leadership, and preserved cultural identity despite modernization.

Etymology & Origins

People of the River

The term "Manobo" is believed to be derived from the word "Man" meaning people and "Suba" meaning river. Thus, Manobo refers to "people of the river," which reflects their early settlements near rivers and forests in Mindanao.

The Manobo are among the earliest inhabitants of Mindanao. They belong to the Austronesian-speaking Indigenous peoples who settled in the island thousands of years ago.

Movement

Migration

The Manobo groups spread across various regions of Mindanao including Bukidnon, Agusan, Davao, Surigao, and Cotabato. Due to conflicts and search for resources, they migrated to mountainous and forested areas.

Resilience

Colonization

During Spanish colonization, the Manobo people resisted outside control and maintained their independence. Later, American colonization introduced education and governance systems, which slowly influenced their traditional way of life.
